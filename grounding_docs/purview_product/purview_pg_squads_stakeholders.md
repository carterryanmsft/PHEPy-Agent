## üìû Escalation Patterns

### Level 1: Squad/PM Contact
- Direct engagement with appropriate PM or squad member
- Use for product questions, DCRs, feature clarifications
- Expected response: 1-2 business days

### Level 2: Squad Leader
- Engagement with squad leader (e.g., Jovin Raj for Support Squad)
- Use for cross-squad coordination, escalations not resolving at L1
- Expected response: 2-3 business days

### Level 3: PG Leadership
- Engagement with Maithili Dandige or other PG leadership
- Use for strategic issues, blocked escalations, executive customer issues
- Expected response: Variable based on severity

### Level 4: Executive
- Engagement with Rohan Kumar
- Use for executive customer escalations, strategic partnerships
- Coordination through PG Leadership required

## ‚úÖ Usage Guidelines for CEM/CARE Team

### When to Use This Document
1. **Escalations**: Determine correct PG contact for product escalations
2. **DCRs**: Identify appropriate PM for feature request submissions
3. **Technical Questions**: Find subject matter experts for specific Purview components
4. **Collaboration**: Understand squad structure for cross-team initiatives
5. **Onboarding**: Orient new team members to PG organizational structure

### Best Practices
- ‚úÖ Start with appropriate squad/PM contact before escalating
- ‚úÖ Reference specific squad or stakeholder when opening escalations
- ‚úÖ Include relevant context (customer, issue type, urgency) when reaching out
- ‚úÖ Use established channels (Teams, email) for initial contact
- ‚úÖ Follow escalation patterns outlined above
- ‚ùå Avoid bypassing appropriate levels unless customer/severity requires it
- ‚ùå Don't duplicate requests across multiple PMs simultaneously
- ‚ùå Don't escalate to executives without PG PM coordination

### Data Freshness
- **Squad Assignments**: Review quarterly or when organizational changes announced
- **PM Assignments**: Validate before major escalations (check internal directory)
- **Contact Information**: Confirm through Teams/Outlook if uncertain
- **Escalation Paths**: Verify through PG leadership if process changes
